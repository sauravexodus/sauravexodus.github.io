FAQ

The questions founders actually ask.

How does a fractional CTO actually work with my team?
Like a part-time executive, not an agency. I join your Slack, the standups that matter, and your board prep. Typically one to two days a week with clear ownership: architecture, hiring, delivery. Your team ships; I make sure it's the right thing, built the right way.
What does it cost?
Less than a bad senior hire. Retainers are scoped by days per week; the due diligence review and the AI MVP Sprint are fixed price. For context, a full-time CTO in the UAE runs AED 400k+ a year before equity. We'll put exact numbers on a 30 minute call.
Do you work remotely? What about timezones?
Most engagements are remote-first. I'm in the UAE (GMT+4): full-day overlap with Europe, comfortable overlap with India, and US East Coast mornings. Dubai and Sharjah teams can have me in the room.
How is this different from hiring a dev agency?
An agency sells hours and owns nothing. I sit on your side of the table. I've been the founding engineer and the founder, so I optimize for the decisions that matter at your stage, including telling you what not to build.
Do you still write code, or just advise?
I ship. Architecture reviews are worth little from someone who stopped coding years ago. Depending on scope, I prototype, pair with your engineers, and review pull requests.
